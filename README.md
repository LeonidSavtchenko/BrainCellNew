        <p>BRAINCELL 1.0 is the ultimate software for modeling and simulating brain cells. With this powerful tool, you can create realistic and interactive models of neurons, synapses, and networks, allowing you to explore the dynamics of brain activity, test hypotheses, and make new discoveries.</p>
        <p>Whether you are a researcher, a student, or simply curious about neuroscience, BRAINCELL 1.0 is the software for you. It's designed to make your brain cells work smarter.</p>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Version 1.0</h2>
        <p>Welcome to our software, "BRAINCELL 1.0," which simulates realistic brain cells, including astrocytes and neurons. This powerful tool is designed to help researchers, neuroscientists, and medical professionals better understand how the brain works and responds to various stimuli and treatments.</p>
        <p>With our software, you can create detailed simulations of individual neurons and astrocytes with nano-geometry details that accurately replicate the complex dynamics of the brain.</p>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Key Features</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Create detailed simulations of brain cells.</li>
            <li>Adjust parameters such as membrane capacitance, ion conductance, and synaptic strength.</li>
            <li>Comprehensive user manual for easy setup and simulation.</li>
            <li>Ongoing support from our team.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Building a Realistic Cell Model</h2>
        <p>The following section describes which experiment data or approximations are preferred when building a realistic cell model:</p>
        <ol>
            <li>A 3D reconstructed tree of main identifiable cell processes importable from <a href="https://neuromorpho.org/">neuromorpho.org</a> in any format.</li>
            <li>Astrocyte nanostructures reconstructed using 3D EM.</li>
            <li>Neuron nanostructures automatically generated by BRAINCELL.</li>
            <li>Average tissue volume fraction occupied by astroglia and neurons.</li>
            <li>The mean membrane surface density and the surface-to-volume fraction values.</li>
            <li>Characteristics like I-V curve and other functional data.</li>
        </ol>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Installation</h2>
        <h3>System Requirements for BRAINCELL:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Languages: C++, MATLAB (not older than 2013), and Neuron 7.0</li>
            <li>Platform: Linux or Windows</li>
            <li>Type of operation: Sequential and parallel (MPI) computing</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Setting up and Running BRAINCELL: Main Regimes of Modeling</h2>
        <ol>
            <li>
                <h4>Constructing Cell Morphology ('Nano-geometry')</h4>
                <p>Import the 3D main-branch morphology of cells from <a href="https://neuromorpho.org/">neuromorpho.org</a> into BRAINCELL; generate nanoscopic cell protrusions within the NEURON environment.</p>
            </li>
            <li>
                <h4>NEURON-Based Simulations of Membrane Mechanisms</h4>
                <p>Adjust BRAINCELL morphology, populate cells with membrane mechanisms, and set up simulation configurations and protocols.</p>
            </li>
            <li>
                <h4>Simulating Full-Scale Ionic Dynamics</h4>
                <p>Design and simulate intracellular and extracellular ionic dynamics within realistic geometry using parallel computing.</p>
            </li>
        </ol>
    </section>
